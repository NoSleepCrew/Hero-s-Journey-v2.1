📖 The Hero's Journey

Killian's Path to Legendary

Every great journey begins with a choice.

On or about 13 June 2026, while riding home in a motor vehicle, Killian made a proposal. In exchange for the purchase of the application known as My Singing Monsters Composer, he promised that he would refrain from requesting additional purchases for a period of two (2) years.

Said statement was heard by:

  • Your Parents

And later repeated to:

  • Two (2) of your Aunts
  • Lindsey
  • Jessica

Because of that promise, your Parents agreed to purchase the application for approximately $4.99.

Over time, it became clear that simply counting down two years (730 days) was less interesting than building a system that rewarded growth, responsibility, perseverance, and good choices.

As a result, this Agreement evolved into a journey.

Through reading, family meals, respectful language, cooperation, and other positive choices, Killian may earn progress toward Hero Levels, rewards, and reductions to the duration of this Agreement.

The ultimate goal of this journey is to achieve Legendary status.

Original Expiration Date: 13 June 2028

ARTICLE I – THE CHALLENGE

What this section means: This article defines the original challenge. It explains that the goal is to practice patience, self-control, and delayed gratification with optional purchases while still allowing ordinary needs, food, family activities, and normal life to continue.

Every hero faces challenges along the road to becoming Legendary.

One of Killian's challenges is learning patience, self-control, and delayed gratification. As part of this journey, Killian agrees to go without requesting optional purchases while this agreement remains in effect.

Therefore, Killian agrees not to ask for, request, hint at, suggest, negotiate for, campaign for, bargain for, lobby for, or otherwise seek the purchase of:

  • Toys
  • Games
  • Apps
  • DLC
  • In-game currency
  • Collectibles
  • Gadgets
  • Subscriptions
  • Mystery boxes
  • Limited editions
  • "Just one small thing"

This challenge is about learning patience and self-control with optional purchases. It is not intended to prevent Killian from asking for ordinary needs or participating in normal family activities.

Therefore, Killian will always be encouraged to request food, drinks, meals, snacks, and other items related to health, nutrition, family activities, celebrations, travel, and everyday life.

Killian agrees not to:

  • Recruit family members or other sympathetic parties.
  • Present advertisements, screenshots, catalogs, videos, wish lists, or similar materials as supporting evidence.
  • Ask one parent after receiving a denial from the other.
  • Reclassify a purchase as educational, necessary, important, or exempt from this Agreement.
  • Employ technical loopholes, semantic arguments, or creative interpretations of the word "asking."

ARTICLE II – COMMON TEMPTATIONS AND EXCUSES

What this section means: This article names common loopholes and arguments ahead of time. The goal is to make the rules clear before the argument happens, not to punish creativity.

Every hero encounters temptations, shortcuts, and clever excuses during a difficult journey.

The following statements may be creative, entertaining, persuasive, or even factually accurate, but they shall not, by themselves, end, suspend, modify, invalidate, or otherwise defeat this challenge:

  • "But this is different."
  • "That doesn't count."
  • "I was joking."
  • "Technically I wasn't asking."
  • "I was only showing it to you."
  • "I just wanted your opinion."
  • "It's only $1."
  • "It's on sale."
  • "Everyone else has one."
  • "I didn't know this existed yet."
  • "I'll never ask for anything ever again."
  • "Mom said maybe."
  • "Dad said maybe."
  • "You didn't say no."
  • "I forgot."
  • "That was Old Me."
  • "I have changed as a person."
  • "The contract is outdated."
  • "I request a retrial."
  • "Hear me out..."
  • "Okay, but..."
  • Any substantially similar argument.

ARTICLE III – THE PATH OF KNOWLEDGE

What this section means: This path rewards finishing books. It is designed to encourage literacy, curiosity, perseverance, and completion of long-term goals. The reward is not just starting a book, but finishing it and being able to talk about it.

Purpose

Every hero gains strength through experience, knowledge, and wisdom. Heroes who wish to reach Legendary status must continually strive to develop all three. Reading allows heroes to learn from places they have never been, people they have never met, and experiences they have never had.

The purpose of this program is to encourage literacy, learning, curiosity, perseverance, and the completion of long-term goals.

Qualifying Books

A qualifying book:

  • Must contain at least fifty (50) pages.
  • Must be completed in its entirety.
  • Must be completed after the Effective Date.

Reading only fifty (50) pages of a longer book does not qualify.

Books may not be divided into multiple books for accounting purposes.

  • For example, a 300-page book does not become 6 50-page books simply because that would be convenient.

A completed book may not be claimed more than once unless specifically approved by your Parents.

Verification

Killian should be able to discuss:

  • Main heroes
  • Plot
  • Major events
  • Ending

The purpose of these questions is not to create a test, but to discuss the book. What did you like? What did you dislike? Would you like another book like this?

Quest Rewards

  • One (1) completed qualifying book = Five (5) day reduction.
  • Every ten (10) completed qualifying books = Additional two (2) day bonus reduction.

Examples:

  • 30 completed books = 150 days of reduction, plus six (6) bonus days, for a total reduction of 156 days.
  • 40 completed books = 200 days of reduction, plus eight (8) bonus days, for a total reduction of 208 days.

Hero Point Milestones

One (1) Hero Point shall be awarded upon completion of each of the following book milestones:

1 completed book11 completed books21 completed books
3 completed books13 completed books23 completed books
5 completed books15 completed books25 completed books
7 completed books17 completed books27 completed books
9 completed books19 completed books29 completed books

After twenty-nine (29) completed books, no additional Hero Points may be earned through the Reading Quest.

A Hero may continue earning Quest Rewards through additional completed books even after all available Reading Hero Points have been earned.

Guiding Principle

Finishing difficult things is often more important than starting them.

Many people begin adventures. Heroes finish them.

ARTICLE IV – THE PATH OF BANQUETING

What this section means: This path rewards participation in family meals and trying foods fairly. The goal is not to force enjoyment. The goal is honest effort, openness, and thoughtful decisions based on experience.

Purpose

Heroes encounter many new experiences on the road to Legendary status. Some of those experiences involve trying new foods, revisiting old foods, and making informed decisions based upon experience rather than assumptions.

The purpose of this program is to encourage participation in family meals and thoughtful food decisions.

Food Principles

Heroes often encounter unfamiliar foods, flavors, and experiences during their journeys. The goal is not to enjoy every food. The goal is to approach new experiences with honesty, curiosity, and an open mind.

  1. People's tastes can change over time.
  2. You are not expected to enjoy every food.
  3. You are entitled to your own opinions regarding food and may honestly decide that you dislike a particular food after giving it a fair chance.
  4. A fair chance means taking three real bites, not three tiny mouse bites.
  5. After taking three real bites, you may honestly state that you do not like the food.
  6. Food should be judged by experience rather than appearance, smell, or expectation.

Requirements

  • Eat the same primary meal being consumed by the family.
  • Applies to home-cooked meals and restaurant meals.
  • No special substitute meals.
  • Participation must be made in good faith.

Meal Merit Points

  1. Each qualifying family meal earns one (1) Meal Merit Point.
  2. Meal Merit Points accumulate continuously and never reset.
  3. Previously earned Meal Merit Points are never lost.

Quest Rewards

Each Meal Merit Point earned reduces the Challenge Duration by one (1) day.

Examples:

  • 10 Meal Merit Points = 10 days of reduction.
  • 50 Meal Merit Points = 50 days of reduction.
  • 100 Meal Merit Points = 100 days of reduction.

Hero Point Milestones

One (1) Hero Point shall be awarded upon reaching each of the following Meal Merit Point milestones:

1060110
2070120
3080130
4090140
50100150

After one hundred fifty (150) Meal Merit Points, no additional Hero Points may be earned through the Family Meals Quest.

A Hero may continue earning Meal Merit Points and reductions to the Challenge Duration even after all available Family Meals Hero Points have been earned.

Guiding Principle

Progress is earned one meal at a time. Missing one opportunity does not erase progress already made.

ARTICLE V – THE PATH OF RESPECT

What this section means: This path rewards respectful communication, self-correction, and continued effort after mistakes. It is not about perfection, and it does not punish honest conversations about difficult feelings.

Purpose

Heroes are not remembered for whether or not they make mistakes; they are remembered for how they respond to them.

The purpose of this quest is to encourage respectful communication, personal responsibility, self-correction, and continued effort after mistakes.

Language Violations

May include:

  • Profanity
  • Verbal Aggression
  • Disrespectful language
  • Negative self-talk
  • Being disrespectful

Talking about difficult feelings, fears, worries, sadness, anger, or thoughts that concern you shall never be considered a Language Violation.

Grace and Self-Correction

A Language Violation shall not automatically disqualify an entire day.

If a Language Violation occurs but is immediately corrected, and a good-faith effort is made to use appropriate language for the remainder of the day, your Parents may still award a Language Merit Day.

The goal of this quest is not perfection. The goal is recognizing mistakes, correcting them, and continuing forward.

Language Merit Days

Killian earns one (1) Language Merit Day for each day in which he makes a good-faith effort to follow the language expectations of this Agreement.

Quest Rewards

  1. Each Language Merit Day reduces the Challenge Duration by one (1) day.
  2. For every fourteen (14) Language Merit Days earned, an additional seven (7) day bonus reduction shall be awarded.

Examples:

  • 14 Language Merit Days = 21 total days of reduction.
  • 28 Language Merit Days = 42 total days of reduction.

Effect of Violations

  • A Language Violation shall not erase previously earned Language Merit Days.
  • A Language Violation shall not automatically prevent future Language Merit Days from being earned.
  • A day containing repeated, intentional, or uncorrected Language Violations will not qualify as a Language Merit Day.

Hero Point Rewards

  1. One (1) Hero Point shall be awarded for every fourteen (14) Language Merit Days earned.

Hero Point Recovery

  1. One (1) Hero Point may be exchanged to convert a non-qualifying day into a qualifying Language Merit Day, subject to parental approval.
  2. No more than one (1) Hero Point may be spent toward Language Merit Day recovery during any single calendar day.
  3. Use of a Hero Point does not guarantee that a day will qualify as a Language Merit Day.
  4. Repeated, intentional, or uncorrected Language Violations occurring after a Hero Point is spent may still result in disqualification of the day.

Guiding Principles

  • Everybody makes mistakes. What matters is what you choose to do next.
  • One mistake does not erase the good choices that came before it, and it does not prevent good choices afterward.
  • You do not lose points for getting annoyed. You lose points based on what you choose to do next.

ARTICLE VI – THE PATH OF BROTHERHOOD

What this section means: This path rewards sincere effort to be a good brother. It is not about never getting annoyed. It is about choosing kindness, patience, teamwork, and self-control even when those choices are difficult.

Purpose

Brothers may not always agree, but great brothers learn how to work through challenges together. A good brother is not someone who never gets annoyed. A good brother is someone who continues trying to be kind, patient, and helpful even when it is difficult.

This path is to encourage kindness, teamwork, patience, and positive interactions with your younger brother.

Examples of Qualifying Behavior

  • Sharing
  • Helping
  • Including brother in activities
  • Solving disagreements peacefully
  • Walking away from unnecessary arguments
  • Showing patience
  • Demonstrating self-control
  • Choosing not to tease when teasing would be easy

Cooperation Credits

Cooperation Credits are awarded when your Parents determine that genuine effort was made.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is to make a sincere effort to be a good brother. Heroes earn recognition for continuing to make good choices even when those choices are difficult.

Quest Rewards

Each Cooperation Credit earned reduces the Challenge Duration by three (3) days.

Examples:

  • 5 Cooperation Credits = 15 days of reduction.
  • 10 Cooperation Credits = 30 days of reduction.
  • 25 Cooperation Credits = 75 days of reduction.

Hero Point Milestones

One (1) Hero Point shall be awarded upon reaching each of the following Cooperation Credit milestones:

515253545
1020304050

After fifty (50) Cooperation Credits, no additional Hero Points may be earned through the Brotherhood Quest.

A Hero may continue earning Cooperation Credits and reductions to the Challenge Duration even after all available Brotherhood Hero Points have been earned.

Guiding Principles

  1. You do not get a Cooperation Credit for being nice and then immediately being mean.
  2. You do not lose points for getting annoyed. You lose points based on what you choose to do next.
  3. Being the bigger person is worth more than being the louder person.
  4. Winning an argument is not the same thing as solving a problem.
  5. If you know it will bother your brother and you do it anyway, it probably does not count as cooperation.

ARTICLE VII – HERO POINT SYSTEM

What this section means: Hero Points are the long-term recognition system. Lifetime Hero Points determine Hero Levels and never decrease. Current Hero Point Balance is what can be spent.

Purpose

Recognize long-term effort, growth, and achievement across multiple areas of life.

Hero Points may be earned through reading, family meals, respectful language, cooperation, and other approved achievements.

Hero Points represent a Hero's growth and progress throughout the journey and may be spent only for approved purposes.

Lifetime Hero Points Earned

Lifetime Hero Points Earned determine Hero Levels and never decrease.

Current Hero Point Balance

Current Hero Point Balance represents Hero Points available for spending.

Spending Hero Points reduces the Current Hero Point Balance but does not reduce Lifetime Hero Points Earned.

Guiding Principle

Good choices made today can help recover from mistakes made tomorrow.

ARTICLE VIII – HERO LEVELS

What this section means: Hero Levels are permanent achievements. Spending Hero Points does not erase a level that has already been earned.

LevelLifetime Hero Points RequiredReward
Apprentice10$10
Adventurer20$20
Scholar30$40
Champion40$80
Legend50$100

Level Rules

  1. Hero Levels are based upon Lifetime Hero Points Earned and not upon your current Hero Point balance.
  2. Each Hero Level may be achieved only once.
  3. Rewards associated with a Hero Level may only be claimed upon the first instance of achieving that Hero Level.
  4. Reaching a previously achieved Hero Level a second time does not entitle you to an additional Hero Level Reward.
  5. Spending Hero Points does not reduce previously earned Hero Levels.
  6. Once achieved, a Hero Level remains achieved. A Hero who has become an Adventurer never becomes less than an Adventurer.

Guiding Principle

A Hero Level represents an achievement, not a balance. Once earned, a Hero Level becomes a permanent part of your journey and may only be earned once.

ARTICLE IX – LEGENDARY CONSIDERATION

What this section means: Legendary Consideration is the possible early-release process. It is available only after Legend status and only as a request for consideration, not as an automatic right.

Purpose

The principle of this challenge is growth, not merely the passage of time.

In extraordinary circumstances, a hero who has demonstrated exceptional responsibility, maturity, perseverance, and character growth may request Legendary Consideration from the Court of Parents.

Legendary Consideration is a privilege, not a right, and approval is never guaranteed.

Eligibility

  1. No petition may be submitted before three (3) months have elapsed from the Effective Date.
  2. Hero must have achieved Legend status to be eligible for consideration.

Requirements

Killian may ask for early release if he demonstrates:

  • Compliance with the spirit and purpose of this Agreement
  • Good-faith participation in family expectations
  • Maturity, responsibility, and self-correction after mistakes
  • Consistent effort throughout the Hero's Journey

Completion of these items allows for consideration but does not guarantee approval.

The burden of proof rests entirely upon The Hero.

ARTICLE X – THE HIGH COURT OF PARENTS

What this section means: This article gives parents authority to interpret the agreement fairly, resolve edge cases, and protect the spirit of the journey when technicalities or loopholes appear.

Purpose

No agreement can anticipate every situation, every question, or every creative argument.

The purpose of the High Court of Parents is to interpret this Agreement fairly, resolve disputes, evaluate petitions for Legendary Consideration, and ensure that the spirit of the Hero's Journey remains more important than technicalities, loopholes, or clever arguments.

Admissible Evidence

When evaluating disputes, questions, claims, appeals, petitions for Legendary Consideration, or other matters arising under this Agreement, the High Court of Parents may consider any evidence it deems relevant, including but not limited to:

  • Witness testimony
  • Screenshots
  • Written notes
  • Family recollections
  • Statements made in the car
  • Historical parental memory

Examples of admissible evidence may include, but are not limited to:

  • "Remember what you said in the car?"
  • "That is not what happened, and you know it."
  • "Your mother and I were both there."

Governing Authority

This Agreement shall be interpreted and administered by the High Court of Parents in accordance with its purpose, spirit, and guiding principles.

Appeals

Appeals may be submitted to the High Court of Parents for review.

Decisions of the High Court of Parents shall be final, subject only to future rulings of the High Court of Parents.
